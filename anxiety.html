Your questions about Anxiety, answered

Anxiety disorders are common mental health conditions that change how you react to situations. If you have an anxiety disorder, you may have feelings of dread or fear when faced with a certain circumstance. This is usually accompanied by physical signs and symptoms of anxiety, such as a racing heart, muscle tension, or trouble sleeping. While it's normal to experience some anxiety in your day-to-day life, having an anxiety disorder can make it hard to function in a healthy way. It can interfere with your ability to respond to a situation calmly and might lead to emotional reactions when you feel triggered by something out of your control. While an anxiety disorder can be overwhelming, there are several treatments available to help you tackle the symptoms.

Anxiety disorders are a group of conditions that share the same symptoms, such as feeling very anxious and scared. There are many different types of anxiety disorders, each with its own set of symptoms. Some of the most common are Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD), Panic Disorder (Panic attacks), Phobias, Separation Anxiety, Social Anxiety Disorder (SAD), Agoraphobia, and Selective Mutism. Other mental health conditions can also have anxiety symptoms, so it's important to talk to your doctor if you're worried about something.

Anxiety disorders are not caused by something you do or something that happened to you. There are many different reasons why someone might develop an anxiety disorder, including things that have happened to them in the past, their genes, and changes in their brain chemistry as a result.

It's not always possible to prevent anxiety disorders, but there are things you can do to reduce the impact of symptoms. For example, managing stress and exercising can help reduce the impact of anxiety on your life.

Psychotherapy can help you work on the various psychological aspects of anxiety, like worrying about things constantly. Psychiatrists can help you work on the various physical aspects of anxiety, like not being able to sleep or having intense panic attacks. Self-care includes all the steps you can take to help yourself in your mental health journey, like being more mindful and learning relaxation techniques. Generally, the best possible outcomes from treatment arise when therapy, self-care, and psychiatry work together as they are all complementary to one another.
