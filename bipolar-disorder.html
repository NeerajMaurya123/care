This is what Bipolar Disorder can look like...

It involves manic episodes, where you may feel the following:

  • Extremely happy
  • Energetic
  • Confident
  • Talkative
  • Impulsive

It also involves depressive episodes, where you may experience:

  • Intense sadness
  • Lack of energy
  • Numbness
  • Lack of concentration
  • Feelings of guilt

Your questions about Bipolar Disorder, answered

Bipolar disorder is a mental health condition in which you will experience changes in mood, energy, ability to sleep, and overall functioning. These changes can be very intense and abrupt, and are caused by a chemical imbalance in the brain. Mania (a more severe form of mood change) and depression (a less severe form of mood change) are two of the major features of this condition.

Bipolar Disorder is a condition where people have episodes of high mood (mania) and low mood (depression). However, it is not necessary for someone to have an episode of depression in order to have bipolar disorder. People with bipolar disorder sometimes have more intense mania episodes that can require hospitalization. People with bipolar II disorder have less intense episodes of mania, but they can still have depressive episodes that last for two weeks or more. People with cyclothymia have episodes of mania and depression that can happen rapidly, for a period of two years or more. If a person does not have an episode of mania or depression, but experiences severe mood swings, it is considered as Unspecified Bipolar Disorder.

Bipolar Disorder is a mental illness that is characterised by alternating periods of high energy and low energy. These periods can be very intense, with people feeling very confident and unstoppable. However, during a depressive episode, people may feel very sad and hopeless. There may also be times where people feel very sleepy and have trouble staying focused. Additionally, people with Bipolar Disorder may become very impulsive, making bad decisions that can hurt them or others.

In a mixed episode, you might experience symptoms of both a manic and depressive episode at the same time. This can make you feel very energetic and restless, as well as sad and negative. Mixed episodes are often a difficult part of living with bipolar disorder, because they can cause a lot of disturbance in your life.
